# Spaghetti Bolognese

A slow-simmered Italian-American meat sauce served over fresh pasta. Best made
ahead — the flavor deepens overnight. Freezes well for up to three months.

The sauce is a riff on the classic Bolognese ragù: soffritto, browned beef,
wine reduction, and a long, gentle simmer. The milk near the end is the
traditional touch — it tenderizes the meat and rounds out the acid.

*italian, pasta, weekend, makes-leftovers*

**6 servings, 1.2 kg sauce**

---

## Sauce

- *500 g* ground beef (80/20)
- *200 g* pancetta, finely diced
- *1* yellow onion
- *2* carrots
- *2 stalks* celery
- *4 cloves* garlic
- *800 g* canned crushed tomatoes
- *2 tbsp* tomato paste
- *250 ml* dry red wine
- *250 ml* whole milk
- *2* bay leaves
- *1 tsp* salt
- *0.5 tsp* black pepper
- *2 tbsp* olive oil

## Pasta and finish

- *600 g* [Fresh egg pasta](./fresh-egg-pasta.md)
- *50 g* parmesan, finely grated
- flat-leaf parsley, for garnish

---

Dice the onion, carrots, and celery into a rough mince (soffritto). Heat the
olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat and add the soffritto with
a pinch of salt. Cook 8–10 minutes until softened and just starting to color.

Add the pancetta and the garlic. Cook another 3 minutes until the pancetta
renders.

Increase the heat to medium-high and add the ground beef, breaking it up with
a wooden spoon. Cook until no pink remains and the meat begins to brown, about
6–8 minutes.

Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute. Pour in the wine and let it
reduce by half, scraping the bottom to release the fond.

Add the crushed tomatoes, the milk, the bay leaves, the salt, and the pepper.
Bring to a bare simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Partially cover and let
cook for at least 90 minutes — 3 hours is better. Stir occasionally and adjust
salt at the end.

While the sauce finishes, cook the pasta to al dente in heavily salted water.
Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.

Toss the drained pasta with about half the sauce in a wide pan, adding pasta
water as needed to loosen. Plate, top with the remaining sauce, the parmesan,
and the parsley.
